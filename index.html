LET'S MAKE SOMETHING TOGETHER!

Hi! I'm George, a software developer in the Hartford, CT area with a background in music and audio. I'm always in search of ways to make things more usable, adaptable, and efficient. The thing that drives me is finding where beauty meets structure.

ABOUT ME

My whole life I have been fascinated by the merging of art and technology. At first I was more heavily focuses on the arts. I went to college at the Hartt School for music composition and jazz piano. In my studies I discovered things like generative music, projection mapping, FFT effects, and a graphical programming language called Max MSP. The more I explored projects in Max, the more I came to understand coding in general, discovering how ubiquitously useful and freeing it is! Several of my projects thus far have been related to music and manipulating sound.

As I continued I became more interested in computers in general and became involved in projects like building and fixing computers and exploring networking protocols while developing a UDP based application for sending music notation between devices for structured improvisation. I began studying IT concepts for the CompTIA A+ certification and passed the first portion in August! After that I took a extremely fun Javascript course on Udemy and have since created a couple of web apps.

CURRENT PROJECTS

RHYTHMODULUM

A web app for generating and manipulating rhythmic patterns. Soon to come: MIDI Clock/Transport control, MIDI IN/OUT, and custom sample kits.

CHORALATOR

A web app that generates music with up to four voices by concatenating randomly generated motifs into larger musical forms. Currently available as an Ableton Live 'M4L' device.

RACQUETSONG

A virtual space in Unity for improvising music through controlled randomness and the physicality of raquetball. Made in collaboration with Sean Nealon.
